SUSTAINABILITY

Sustainability is one of the core principles that we attempt to uphold here at Plate of Africa. We understand that sustainability is the backbone to not just our restaurant but to the world as a whole. Through the lens of a plant-based approach, we can reduce our carbon footprint greatly, as the resources required to grow plants are much less than that of animal products. By implementing a vegan menu, we greatly decrease the amount of greenhouse gas we emit, water we use, and cut down on deforestation.

While this is all good in theory, it is important to acknowledge the fact that in order to enact real change, we need to take action. Despite other restaurants claiming that they are environmentally-friendly, Plate of Africa takes real steps in order to create real change. We partner with local farmers to provide the freshest ingredients and use seasonal produce in order to cut down on our emissions. This ultimately means that the menu changes with the seasons, showing our commitment to a healthier planet and brighter future.

  • 02 Local Sourcing

    Local Sourcing Benefits

    We source over 80% of our ingredients from farms within a 100-mile radius of our restaurant. This approach offers numerous benefits:

    • Reduced carbon footprint from transportation
    • Support for local farmers and the local economy
    • Fresher, more nutritious produce that hasn't traveled for days
    • Preservation of regional food traditions and biodiversity

    By choosing local, we ensure that your meal not only tastes better but also contributes to a more sustainable food system.

  • 03 Direct Shipping

    Direct Farm Relationships

    We've eliminated middlemen by establishing direct relationships with our farmers. This approach allows us to:

    • Ensure fair compensation for farmers' hard work
    • Reduce food waste through precise ordering
    • Maintain quality control from farm to kitchen
    • Create custom growing plans for unique African vegetables

    These partnerships enable us to bring you authentic African flavors while supporting sustainable farming practices.

  • 04 Seasonal Menus

    Seasonal Menu Adaptation

    Our menu evolves with the seasons, reflecting what's currently growing in our region. This seasonal approach offers:

    • Peak flavor and nutritional value from in-season produce
    • Exciting menu variations throughout the year
    • Support for natural growing cycles and biodiversity
    • Creative fusion of African traditions with local seasonal ingredients

    Each visit to Plate of Africa offers a unique experience that celebrates the best of what's currently available from our farm partners.

OUR PROCESS

STEP 1

Harvesting Fresh Ingredients

Our Process begins with harvesting only the freshest ingredients. We partner with our local farms to provide all of the ingredients that our found in our dishes, such us:

  • Arugula
  • Hibiscus Leaves
  • Berbere (Ethiopian Spice)
  • Ube (Purple African Fruit)
  • Egusi Seeds (African Melon Seeds)
  • Teff (Ethiopian Grains)

These farmers do more than just collect ingredients; they are critical partners in Plate of Africa's mission to a more sustainable, ethical, and culturally diverse future.

STEP 2

Transporting Sustainably

After the collection of ingredients, Plate of Africa begins the process of transporting resources. The transportation of ingredients across continents emits a large quantity of carbon emissions, which negatively affects our atmosphere. However, in order to combat this, Plate of Africa sources 100% of its ingredients from farms within 100 miles from our locations, overall contributing to a 99.44% decrease in total carbon emissions from transportation.

STEP 3

Culinary Expertise

Our next step in our process is the preparation of our dishes. We take pride in maintaining a high level of quality, as well as cultural authenticity when it comes to our dishes. Notable dishes include our:

STEP 4

Served To Your Table

Dishes arrive at your table, for our wonderful customers to enjoy. It is the sharing of dishes that allows the spread of African culture, and the creation of a diverse community!

STEP 5

Farming Forward

The last – and often forgotten – step of our process is the composting of our waste. At the Plate of Africa, we believe in giving back to the land that nourishes us. We compost food scraps and waste to enrich the soil, helping local farms grow the next generation of fresh, sustainable ingredients. By closing the loop, we contribute to a more sustainable, healthier food system, ensuring that every meal served leaves a positive impact on our community and environment.

NOURISHMENT FOR THE BODY AND SOUL

Veganism is not simply about care for the environment, but also care for the individual. At Plate of Africa, we want to ensure that our diners are eating high-quality vegan food that will be a great benefit to their health. The dishes we serve are not only packed with flavor, but with nutrients as well. Plant-based diets are growing in popularity among health groups due to its many physical and psychological benefits.
