    • OUR APPROACH TO VEGANISM

    SUSTAINABILITY

    Sustainability is one of the core principles that we attempt to uphold here at Plate of Africa. We understand that sustainability is the backbone to not just our restaurant, but to the world as a whole. Through the lens of a plant-based approach, we can reduce our carbon footprint greatly, as the resources required to create plants are much less than that of animal products. By implementing a vegan menu, we greatly decrease the amount of greenhouse gas we emit, water we use, and cut down on deforestation.


    While this is all good in theory, it is important to acknowledge the fact that in order to enact real change, we need to take action. Despite other restaurants claiming that they are environmentally-friendly, Plate of Africa takes real steps in order to create real change. We partner with local farmers to provide the freshest ingredients, and use seasonal produce in order to cut down on our emissions. This ultimately means that the menu changes with the seasons, showing our commitment to a healthier planet.

    FROM FARM TO TABLE

    Our process from farm to table begins with our farmers. We have long-standing relationships with the local farmers in the area, certifying that the ingredients present in the kitchen are that of the highest quality. From our legumes to our hearty greens, all ingredients have been carefully selected with our patrons’ palette in mind. From the farm, our food is directly shipped towards our locations, with no shipping lasting longer than two days, in order to ensure prompt delivery, freshness, and quality. These ingredients are directly used in our kitchen, with our highly-trained staff highlighting its natural flavor and intensity. In order to prioritize freshness and sustainability, our menu shifts with the seasons, guaranteeing maximum quality. After this, our chefs create the culinary masterpieces that you see before you today. This model of farm-to-table dining not only prioritizes quality, but also our commitment to sustainable business, economic, and environmental practices. Through this transparency, we hope you feel proud eating at Plate of Africa, knowing that every bite is from that of ethical farming and environmental wellness in mind.


    NOURISHMENT FOR THE BODY AND SOUL


    Veganism is not simply about care for the environment, but also care for the individual. The dishes we served are not only packed with flavor, but of nutrients as well. A plant-based diet is a diet that is growing in popularity among medical groups due to its vast physical and psychological benefits. Plant-based diets are typically higher in Vitamins C, D, and A than that of meat-based diets, contributing to healthier bones, skin, and a stronger immune system.
